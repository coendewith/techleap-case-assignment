# State of Dutch Tech Patterns & Insights

This document extracts key patterns and insights from the State of Dutch Tech reports (2024-2025) to inform the startup ecosystem analysis.

## VC Investment Trends

### Investment Volume
- **2023 Drop**: VC investment dropped ~25% in 2023 for Dutch tech, similar to European trends
- **Domestic Investor Behavior**: Domestic investors pulling back on large later-stage rounds
- **Recovery Patterns**: Need to analyze if this is cyclical or structural

### Investment Focus Areas
- **Deeptech Growth**: Deeptech segment (AI, quantum, advanced manufacturing) has grown significantly
- **Scale-up Ratios**: Deeptech ventures have higher scale-up ratios
- **Value Creation**: Deeptech increasingly important in overall value creation

## Sector Analysis

### Strong Sectors
1. **Health & Biotech**: Strong concentration of Dutch startups
2. **Semiconductors**: Growing importance in Dutch tech ecosystem
3. **Enterprise Software**: Significant presence
4. **Clean Technology**: Strategic priority area

### Sector Characteristics
- **Biotech**: High capital requirements, longer development timelines, strong investor appetite
- **Software**: Faster paths to revenue, more companies but lower average funding
- **Deeptech**: Higher scale-up ratios, longer investment cycles

## Geographic Clusters

### Dominant Regions
- **North Holland**: Major hub for startup/scale-up activity
- **South Holland**: Significant concentration
- **North Brabant**: Important regional cluster

### Regional Patterns
- Geographic concentration in western Netherlands
- Urban vs regional distribution patterns
- Connection to universities and research institutions

## Talent & Skills Gaps

### Digital Skills
- **Basic Digital Skills**: 82.7% of population have basic digital skills (above EU average)
- **ICT Specialists**: Shortage of sufficient numbers of ICT specialists
- **Gender Imbalance**: Persistent gender imbalance in tech sector

### Enterprise Technology Adoption
- **AI/Advanced Tech**: Adoption lags peer countries somewhat
- **Smaller Firms**: Especially slower adoption among smaller firms
- **Digital Transformation**: Room for improvement in enterprise digital transformation
